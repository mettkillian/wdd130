Care Tips

There are many do’s and don’ts in caring for your multi-layer clear-coat painted sheet molded compound (SMC) body panels. Paint corrections are costly and complete paint restoration is the most expensive part of putting new life back into your car. It is critical that care be taken to not etch the chrome finishes or cloud the clearcoat with spiral paint damage. The LS1 Engine

In 1997, the LS1 engine was the brainchild of Chevrolet engineers. It replaced the powerful but inefficent LT4 engine that failed to meet the new emissions restrictions put in place by the Federal government. Refusing to abandon the small-block push rod design that put Chevrolet at the top of the performance V8 chain for more than 30 years, Chevrolet Engineers determined to stick with the small-block design but give it an all new all aluminum block and heads, new intake, ingnition, and ECM tune to produce an unheard of 345 horsepower and 350 ft pounds of torque while still meeting California and Federal Emission standards. It was the beginning of the LS era, the most widely swapped and beloved small-block engine of all time.
